📰 Cisco Newsletter Automation

Weekly Workflow That Saves 3-4 Hours

The Big Picture: Automate the entire newsletter creation process so you spend 10 minutes reviewing instead of 3-4 hours writing.

How It Works

🕕 Sunday Evening - Automation Runs

1 Trigger (6:00 PM)
A timer wakes up the system automatically every Sunday.
2 Tavily Search (6:05 PM)
Searches the internet for latest Cisco news about Security, Networking, and AI.
3 Cupid Writes (6:10 PM)
AI creates 2-3 executive summaries, each 10-15 sentences long.
4 Save to Notion (6:15 PM)
Stores the newsletter in an organized database for easy reference.
5 Slack Notification (6:15 PM)
Sends you a message: "Draft is ready for review!"

🕘 Monday Morning - You Take Over

6 You Review (8:00 AM)
Read the draft and make minor edits if needed (takes ~10 minutes).
7 Distribute (9:00 AM)
Send the final newsletter to the Distribution Sales Team.

Security & Control

  • Data stays in your accounts — All information remains in your Tavily, Notion, and Slack accounts
  • You control all content — Review and edit before anything is sent
  • Can pause anytime — Stop the automation whenever you want
  • Nothing shared externally — No sensitive data leaves your systems

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need technical skills to use this?
A: No! The automation runs in the background. You just review and distribute.

Q: What if I want to change something?
A: You have full control. Edit the draft however you like before sending.

Q: Can I pause the automation?
A: Yes, anytime. Just let Cupid know.

Q: What if there's no big news one week?
A: The system will find 2-3 relevant topics, or you can skip that week.
